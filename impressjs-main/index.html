Brief Introduction




Rabindranath Tagore, born May 7, 1861, in Kolkata, India—died August 7, 1941. Bengali poet, short-story writer, music composer, playwright, novelist, and painter brought new prose and verse forms and colloquial language into Bengali literature, freeing it from standard methods based on classical Sanskrit. He was a major figure in conveying Indian culture to the West and vice-versa. He is often recognised as the greatest creative person of early twentieth-century India. He was the first non-European to earn the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913.

Rabindranath Tagore’s Early Life

  • Debendranath Tagore married Sarada Devi and had their youngest child, Rabindranath Tagore, on May 7, 1861, in Calcutta.
  • Dwarkanath Tagore, a rich landowner and social reformer, was Rabindranath's grandfather.
  • Debendranath Tagore led the Brahmo Samaj, a revolutionary religious movement in 19th-century Bengal.
  • Tagore family excelled in literature, theater, and music.
  • Rabindranath had elder brothers: Dwijendranath (poet) and Satyendranath (first Indian in the Indian Civil Service), and Jyotirindranath (writer, composer, musician).

Education

  • Rabindranath Tagore was not enthusiastic about formal education in conventional schools and eventually abandoned his law studies at University College in London.
  • In 1878, he traveled to England to become a barrister, fulfilling his father's wish.
  • Instead, he pursued independent study and immersed himself in the works of Shakespeare.
  • Tagore also explored English, Irish, and Scottish literature and music during his time in England.
  • After his return to India, he married Mrinalini Devi.

Rabindranath Tagore At Shantiniketan

  • Rabindranath Tagore founded "Visva Bharati University" at Shantiniketan.
  • His students called him "Gurudev" out of reverence.
  • The Tagore family played a role in creating Santiniketan, and Rabindranath had a deep connection with the place.
  • Tagore wrote poems and songs while at Shantiniketan.
  • Visva Bharati" University had an inclusive approach, welcoming motivated students from all backgrounds
  • Classes at the university were not confined to classrooms but often held outdoors under the shade of banyan trees.
  • This tradition of conducting classes in open areas continues at Visva Bharati University.

Nationalism And Rabindranath Tagore

  • Rabindranath Tagore was politically astute and outspoken, criticizing both the British rulers and the mistakes of his fellow Bengalis and Indians.
  • He expressed his views through satirical writings about society and politics.
  • Tagore refused a knighthood from the British as a protest against the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre.
  • He was not interested in fame, celebrity status, or wealth for himself; his main concern was the well-being of his country.
  • Rabindranath had a deep affection for his nation.

Contributions of Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore began writing at an early age. His work consists of poems, novels, short stories, dramas, paintings, drawings, and music. The following are a few of his works:

  • Novel:
    ‘Noukadubi’, ‘Gora’, ‘Chaturanga’, ‘Ghare Baire’, and ‘Jogajog’.
  • Short Stories:
    ‘Kabuliwala’, ‘Atottju’, and ‘Haimanti’.
  • Poems:
    ‘Balaka’, ‘Purobi’, ‘Sonar Tori’ and ‘Gitanjali’.

Nobel Prize

  • Rabindranath Tagore won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1913 for his collection Gitanjali published in London in 1912.

Death

Rabindranath Tagore died on August 7, 1941, at the age of 80. According to numerous credible reports, he died of prostate cancer. His death brought an end to a long period of agony. He died in the Jorasanko mansion, where he had grown up. While he might not be there with us, he continues to remain in our hearts and will always be remembered through his incredible works.
